Sign Language Recognition

Break the Silence with Technology


Our Sign Language Recognition system harnesses the power of computer vision and machine learning to interpret American Sign Language (ASL) gestures in real-time.

Designed to bridge the communication gap between the hearing and the deaf or hard-of-hearing community, this tool converts hand signs into readable text instantly. No wearables or special equipment required — just your hands and a webcam. Click below to experience how technology can make communication more inclusive and accessible for everyone.

Understanding the Language of Hands

American Sign Language (ASL) is a complete, natural language that has the same linguistic properties as spoken languages, with grammar that differs from English. The image here represents common ASL signs that form the foundation of communication for millions. By teaching machines to recognize these gestures, we take a step toward seamless interaction and accessibility in public spaces, education, and technology platforms.

Learn the ASL Alphabet

Sign language is a powerful tool that bridges the communication gap between hearing and non-hearing individuals. The image above represents the American Sign Language (ASL) fingerspelling chart — a foundational step in learning sign language. Each hand gesture corresponds to a letter in the English alphabet, enabling users to spell words and names when specific signs are not known.

About This Project

This is a student-led project focused on accessibility, combining AI and gesture recognition to improve digital inclusion. Built with React, Node, and MongoDB for an end-to-end experience.
